This page is dedicated to providing information about the history of old processors. The goal of the website is to improve knowledge of such processors.

The 8086 processor was developed in the late 1970s to much success. It was the processor used in many personal computers of the 1980s. A variant of the processor was used in the construction of the first IBM PC. This processor is where we get the x86 architecture from.

The Zilog Z80, on the other hand, is famous for its use in video game machines from the 1980s, and even the TI-83 graphing calculator. Its impact on the history of computers is immeasurable.
